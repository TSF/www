News from the Factory Floor

January 2010

  • Happy New Year! We're starting the year with a special 2 month period, dedicated entirely to all of our project.
  • Our lightning talk proposal for FOSDEM 2010 was accepted. We'll be introducing UmlCanvas on Sunday at 16:00. We hope to meet you there.
  • Trying to improve the experience of UmlCanvas on all browsers and platforms, we've launched Test UmlCanvas, a site where you can help us to gather input to make all diagrams look the same in all conditions. It only takes a minute, so please submit your results today.
  • We've released the official site for our webUML initiative. Read our rationale and find out what you can do to realise a more social modelling experience.

December 2009

  • We've release a major update to the UmlCanvas website. It's now running a customised Mediawiki, which allows us to add content more easily. Next up is a revamp of the Hosted UmlCanvas site and integrate both sites to create a far better experience.

November 2009

  • We attended Devoxx09. We had the opportunity to talk to a few people and discuss Hosted UmlCanvas. New ideas to further expand the possibilities of UmlCanvas were explorer and the general idea to submit a proposal for next year's conferences seems very likely.

October 2009

  • Based on UmlCanvas, we are currently slowly rolling out a new online tool, called Hosted UmlCanvas, enabling everyone to create, share and include UML diagrams in HTML based content.

September 2009

  • Canvas2D has been updated to 0.3
  • UmlCanvas has been updated to 0.2

July 2009

  • Time to introduce our latest project: ProtoJS
  • Abstract Domain Language has been updated to 0.3

June 2009

  • We'll be at CodeGeneration Conference 2009 in Cambridge. We will present a lightning talk or host a BoF. So maybe we run into each other.
  • A transcript of our presentation at CodeGeneration Conference 2009 is now online on our public wiki.
  • Abstract Domain Language has been updated to 0.2

May 2009

  • What better moment to update the look of our sites, starting with our main site right here...
  • ... and a more marketing-style site for UmlCanvas ;-)
